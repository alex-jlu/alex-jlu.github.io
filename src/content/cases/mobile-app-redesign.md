## Overview

This project involved a full redesign of a consumer mobile application in the health and wellness space. Details have been generalised. My role spanned the entire design process, with particular focus on bridging design and engineering during implementation.

## Problem

The existing app had accumulated technical and design debt over multiple product iterations. The navigation structure was confusing for new users, onboarding drop-off was high, and the visual language was inconsistent across screens. The engineering team was also struggling to implement designs accurately due to poor specification and a lack of reusable components in Figma.

## Approach

### Discovery

I conducted a series of user interviews and reviewed existing analytics and support ticket data to understand where users were getting stuck. Key pain points clustered around onboarding, the home screen information hierarchy, and the navigation model.

### Design

Working from the insights gathered, I reframed the information architecture and tested a new navigation model with a small group of existing users. Once the structure was validated, I moved into high-fidelity design in Figma, building out a component library specific to the mobile context.

Prototyping was central to this project. I built interactive prototypes in Figma for key flows — onboarding, core feature usage, and settings — and used these for both usability testing and engineering handoff.

### Handoff and implementation

Rather than a traditional "hand off and disappear" approach, I stayed closely involved during the build phase. I worked directly with iOS and Android engineers to clarify intent, review builds against the design, and make real-time design decisions when unexpected constraints emerged.

## Reflections

The most valuable part of my involvement was staying embedded with engineering during the build. Design decisions that look obvious on a static screen often need interpretation in the context of motion, performance constraints, and platform conventions. Being present for those conversations improved both the output quality and the team dynamic considerably.
